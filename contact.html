Revalation

Because this is a simple project, I will provide a brief introduction of my life over the first year of my college. These are the things I do, and I believe most students of my age don't. I'd like to say it's the definition of "self-disciplined."

Habit

I hit the gym usually five days a week. It's a great way to relax.

I also often go jogging at the Quanfu sport field during training breaks. It's one of my ways to relax, seeing people join together. Some are playing football, while most of them are taking good care of their bodies.

Every now and then, my cram school teacher gives me some articles from The New York Times. I learn a lot about what's happening in the world. Every part of this globe has its own story going on. Sometimes it's about environmental issues, politics, aging population, or writer strikes (recently in the USA). For me, it's not just about learning English, but a way to have a look at the world and think about what each issue exactly means to us.

Interest

I love listening to podcasts. One of my favorite podcasters is Conan O'Brien, the former late-night show host of TBS. He is the funniest, most talented, and fantastic person I've ever seen in my life. It is him who has taught me a lot of English, and more importantly, some life lessons that no one else could ever teach through his eventful, roller-coaster life journey. I love learning English because of him. His charisma on the show is also quite well-known.

The fact that I love foreigners

Firstly, I want to introduce ISA (International Student Association), a club where I have met so many interesting foreigners from all around the world. It's fortunate for me to get to talk to them and know more about their stories. Sometimes we hang out together and enjoy the distinct foreign cultures. It also strikes me that the fear of talking to foreigners that Taiwanese people have held for so many years has been an ingrained terror. What a pity! We could have had a glimpse of the terrific beauty of people from different countries. I know it's about education and cultural differences, but I believe there must be solutions to solve it, and technology is one of them.

Book taht I love reading again and again

The Ride OF A LIFETIME

This was once the #1 New York Times bestseller written by the CEO of the Walt Disney Company. I read this book during winter vacation last semester. What an amazing book that contains once-in-a-lifetime stories and lessons taken from all kinds of events and from people that the author, Robert Iger, had dealt with! I've always believed that stories are the best teachers in terms of life lessons, and this book proves it. What I observed is that he had great habits since his youth: getting up early, talking with his dad about international issues, working out regularly, and so on.

Because of my lack of vocabulary, it has been troublesome reading about his life while constantly checking the meanings of words. So, I decided to read it again to review his eventful life and philosophy.
